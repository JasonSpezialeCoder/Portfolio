Rob Zombie Directs New Film The Munters

Meagen Surowiecki September 5, 2022

Before his metal rock days, Rob Zombie always watched The Munsters as a child. Once he had the opportunity to remake the sitcom, he went straight to recreating the show into an original film, which will be premiering on Netflix on September 27. The film took almost two years to make, 1313 Mockingbird Lane comes alive by building a replicant Munster home from the ground up in Budapest, Hungary. Giving his fans construction updates on social media of the house being built. The construction took a year to build, which included a few problems and setbacks along the way. It was an exciting moment once the first trailer was released to the public.

Title 1

The Munsters will be a comedy-horror film about the origin of Herman and Lily's crazy courtship unfolding as becoming The Munsters on a hauntingly hilarious trip from Transylvania to Hollywood. It wouldn’t be The Munsters without Grandpa. “Grandpa, nevermore.” Rob Zombie is known for graphic, bloody horror movies such as House of a Thousand Corpses, The Devil's Rejects, Lords of Salem, and his remake of Halloween. For the first time in his directing career, he wrote a comedy/ love story. The Munsters Netflix film is based on the 60s black and white sitcom (1964-1966). Although the show aired for only two seasons, it remains a memorable sitcom for generations. Doctor Frankenstein’s monster will remain one of the greatest dads in sitcom history.

Title 2

The Munsters movie will star Daniel Roebuck as Grandpa, Jeff Daniel Phillips as Herman Munster, and Sheri Moon Zombie as Lily Munster. Together they share a passion for horror and the original sitcom, it will be movie magic. For almost two decades, Jeff Daniel Phillips, Rob Zombie, and his wife Sheri have worked on countless past films, from 3 From Hell, 31, and Lords of Salem. The Munsters movie is the first film in three years Rob Zombie has written, produced, and directed. This will be his ninth film as a director overall. On September 27, along with the premiere on Netflix, the release of The Munsters movie will be released on Blue-Ray DVD that includes bonus features and over sixty minutes from the mind of Rob Zombie. The film includes Jorge Garcia, Richard Brake, Catherine Schell, Sylvester McCoy, and Cassandra Petersen, aka Elvira, plus original cast member of the sitcom The Munsters Butch Patrick as Tin Can Man.

Title 3

In an interview, Rob Zombie talks about his decision to make the film in color to Entertainment Weekly. "I knew that if I went in and demanded, 'This movie's going to be in black-and-white or forget it!' we would not be talking about the Munsters right now because it would have never happened. But what I did was make the colors sort of hyper-real. I noticed when the actors were in their makeup, and they were just walking around, getting lunch or whatever, they looked like cartoon characters come to life," he continued. "They were just so insanely colorful. I was like, I have to light the movie in the same fashion. It seemed like a live-action cartoon, which was exciting. Since Halloween is rapidly approaching, I thought it was the perfect time to MEET THE MUNSTERS! Direct from the set in good old Hungary, I present Herman, Lily, and The Count sitting in front of the newly completed 1313 Mockingbird Lane."
